Artifact 1 – The Evolution of Artificial Intelligence

This artifact explores the evolution of Artificial Intelligence (AI), tracing its journey from early symbolic systems to modern deep learning and generative models. The objective is to understand AI’s progression and highlight key milestones that shaped its transformation into today’s intelligent systems.

The work discusses foundational concepts such as Alan Turing’s 1950 test of machine intelligence and the 1956 Dartmouth Conference that marked the birth of AI as an academic field. It further analyzes the “AI Winters,” when funding and progress slowed, and the breakthroughs that reignited AI research in the 2000s through machine learning and data-driven methods.

In recent years, neural networks and deep learning have revolutionized AI, allowing it to excel in fields like computer vision, natural language processing, and generative AI. The emergence of large-scale models demonstrates both AI’s potential and the ethical responsibility that comes with it.

This exploration emphasizes how leadership and innovation have continuously shaped AI—from theoretical curiosity to a powerful tool transforming industries today.
